    Course Description
    🔗
    An examination of the way the cell accesses, uses, and maintains the information stored in the genome. It begins with an overview of DNA structure and topology, continues with replication, transcription, and translation, and concludes with gene regulation. Special topics could include cancer, stem cells, and RNAi, and focusing on the structure-function relationships of the molecules involved in these processes. Students are required to enroll in the corresponding laboratory (3 hours). Corequisite: BIOL 262L Molecular Biology Lab Prerequisites: BIOL 135 Foundations of Biological Science with Lab, and CHEM 108 Principles of Chemistry and Quantitative Analysis with Lab; or permission of the instructor 1.250 Credit hours 4.000 Lecture hours
    🔗
    Learning Objectives
    🔗
    1. Remember the names and functions of biomolecules involved in the Central Dogma of Biology
    🔗
    2. Understand how each of those molecules accomplish their goal
    🔗
    3. Apply this your knowledge of these molecules to how other related molecules work
    🔗
    4. Analyze molecular mechanisms and describe why each molecule is vital to the process
    🔗
    5. Evaluate how effective molecular mechanisms are and hypothesize why they function this way evolutionarily
    🔗
    6. Create new experiments and presentations based on the knowledge obtained in this course
    🔗

    Structure Quiz
    🔗
    In molecular biology, we will be talking extensively about the structure – function relationships of biological molecules. In order to be able to have these in-depth conversations, we need to all know the structures of the fundamental building blocks of nucleic acids and proteins. As such, we will have a quiz on which you will be tested on the:
    🔗
    Names and abbreviations of all amino acids and nucleic acids
    🔗
    Structures of all amino acids (the full structures and the R groups)
    🔗
    Structures of all nucleotides (including all phosphate, sugar, and base components)
    🔗
    This quiz will include:
    🔗
    Drawing structures of amino acids and nucleic acids
    🔗
    Labeling amino acids and nucleic acids
    🔗
    Using any abbreviations for these structures that is requested.

    3 Minute Thesis
    🔗
    The 3 Minute Thesis (3MT) is a recent phenomenon where students summarize the entirety of their thesis work into the span of three minutes. This may sound like a very easy task, but you must fully understand the background, importance, and molecular biology in order to fully convey a topic in 3 minutes!
    🔗
    The goal of this project is twofold:
    🔗
    Work on succinct, oral scientific communication and public speaking
    🔗
    Learn to apply the information that we learned this semester to new molecules / pathways
    🔗
    Your topic must focus on the molecular biology of something that we have not covered in this course.
    🔗
    You will have exactly 3 minutes and 1 static slide to present your topic.
    🔗
    We will use one lab period to practice 3MT presentations
    🔗
